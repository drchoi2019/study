Case Study: Functional Division of the Uterus (Nuzibao)

Ancient people were aware of the anatomical structure of the uterus but did not include it as one of the Zangfu. Instead, its functions are divided and managed by the 'Liver' (Gan) and 'Kidney' (Shen). Consequently, menstrual issues are mainly treated within the Liver (Gan) domain of Zangxiang Theory, while fertility issues are approached via the Kidney (Shen) domain. This clearly demonstrates TCM's characteristic of viewing the uterus's functions through the lens of the Five Elements and Zangfu.

Liver (Gan): Governs Menstrual Function (Primary Treatment Domain)
Kidney (Shen): Governs Reproductive Function (Infertility Treatment Domain)
